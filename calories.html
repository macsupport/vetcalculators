Calorie Calculation

1. Use Slider to choose specific BCS (Body Condition Score) of pet.
2. An ideal weight is calculated based on the current weight and BCS
3. Results are the approximate daily caloric requirement of pet based on the ideal weight and Resting Energy Requirement (RER) multiplied by the criteria multiplier. (Automatically calculated)
4. Then Enter the calories in 1 cup dry food or 1 can of wet food to calculate the number of cups or cans of food to feed per day based on the ideal weight.
5. See Pet food diet tables

Calories

Even when taking into account a pet’s lifestyle, age, activity level, etc., it is impossible to mathematically determine exactly how many kilocalories a pet needs. Variations in metabolic rates can alter this figure by as much as 20 percent. Results need to be viewed as an estimate only.

Calorie “calculators” or tables cannot take into account what might make an animal’s situation unique.

  • Resting Energy Requirement (RER) = 70 (body weight in kg)^0.75
  • Maintenance Energy Requirement (MER) = appropriate multiplier x RER

Commonly Used Multipliers:

typical neutered pet 1.6
typical intact pet 1.8
weight loss 1
weight gain 1.7
light work 2
moderate work 3
heavy work 6
growth (less than 4 months old) 3
growth (more than 4 months old) 2

Here's what the calculations look like for a neutered pet dog weighing 45 pounds that is at his or her optimum weight.

  • 20.5 kg
  • 70 x 20.5^0.75 = 674 kcal/day
  • 1.6 x 672 = 1075 kcal/day

Remember, this is estimate. This pet’s actual needs may actually be anywhere between 860 kcal/day and 1,290 kcal/day.
